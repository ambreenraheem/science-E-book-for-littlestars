Chapter 08

Our Amazing Body 🫀

Your body is the most incredible machine ever made! Let's explore the systems that keep you alive and thriving every day.

🫀 Let's Discover

The Human Body

Your body is made of trillions of cells, hundreds of bones, and dozens of organs — all working together perfectly every second of your life!

🦴

The Skeletal System

Your body has 206 bones (babies have about 270 — they fuse together as you grow!). Bones give your body shape, protect your organs, and make blood cells in the bone marrow. Joints where bones meet allow movement.

💪

The Muscular System

You have over 600 muscles! They work in pairs — one contracts, the other relaxes. Your heart is a special muscle that never stops working. The smallest muscle is in your ear; the largest is the gluteus maximus (your bottom!).

🫀

The Circulatory System

Your heart pumps blood through 100,000 km of blood vessels — enough to circle Earth 2.5 times! Red blood cells carry oxygen; white blood cells fight germs. Your heart beats about 100,000 times every day!

🧠

The Nervous System

Your brain is the control centre — it receives signals from everywhere and sends instructions back. Nerves carry these signals at up to 430 km/h! The brain uses 20% of all your body's energy, even while sleeping.

💭
Think About It!

Right now, your body is doing millions of things you're completely unaware of — digesting food, making new blood cells, repairing damaged cells, regulating temperature, and even growing! Your body works harder than any machine ever built. 🦾

Respiratory System

Every breath you take fills your lungs with air. Oxygen passes through tiny air sacs (alveoli) into the blood, while carbon dioxide passes out. You breathe about 20,000 times per day! During exercise your breathing rate increases to supply more oxygen to your muscles.

🍽️

Digestive System

Food travels 9 metres through your digestive system! It starts in your mouth (chewing + saliva), goes down the oesophagus, into the stomach (acid breaks it down), through the small intestine (nutrients absorbed) and large intestine (water absorbed), finishing at the rectum. This journey takes 24-72 hours!

🫀

Heart & Blood

Your fist-sized heart is the hardest-working muscle in your body. It has 4 chambers: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium and left ventricle. Blood picks up oxygen in the lungs, delivers it to the body, then returns to the heart — a complete circuit every 20 seconds!

🦴

Bones & Joints

Bones are not dead — they're living tissue filled with blood vessels and nerves! Bone marrow produces 2 million red blood cells every second. Joints allow different types of movement: ball-and-socket (shoulder — rotates), hinge (knee — bends one way), and pivot (neck — rotates).

🧠

Your Amazing Brain

The brain has 86 billion neurons (nerve cells) connected by trillions of synapses. It processes information, stores memories, controls emotions, and runs your body — all simultaneously! Sleep is crucial for your brain to sort memories and repair itself. Never underestimate a good night's sleep!

🤯 WOW! Your small intestine is about 6 metres long — but its inner surface area is 250 square metres (the size of a tennis court!) because of millions of tiny finger-like projections called villi. This huge surface area allows nutrients to be absorbed extremely efficiently!
